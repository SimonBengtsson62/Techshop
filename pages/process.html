How we work

Our Process

No black box, no surprises. Every engagement follows the same disciplined playbook — refined across 120+ projects and 6 years.

Week 1
01
Discover
We go deep before writing a single line of code

The first week is entirely discovery. We embed in your business — talking to users, auditing your codebase, mapping every constraint, and understanding the competitive landscape. Most teams are surprised by what we find. The goal: a shared truth we can build on.

◈
Technical Audit
A forensic review of existing code, architecture, and debt
◉
User Interviews
5–10 interviews with real users to surface hidden problems
⬡
Constraint Map
All technical, legal, and business constraints documented
⬟
Problem Statement
A single agreed-upon definition of what we're solving
Week 2–3
02
Architect
Systems designed to last five years

From requirements to a battle-tested architecture. We write the technical specification your team will reference for years — stack decisions, data models, API contracts, security model, and infrastructure design. No handwavy diagrams. Real decisions, documented.

◎
Tech Spec
Complete technical specification with every major decision justified
⬠
System Diagrams
Infrastructure, data flow, and API architecture diagrams
◈
Data Models
Entity-relationship diagrams and schema definitions
◉
Security Model
Authentication, authorization, and compliance architecture
Week 4+
03
Build
Working software every Friday

Two-week sprints. Daily async updates in Slack. Working demos every Friday — no slide decks, no "we're making progress" updates. You see real software, on your staging environment, every week. Priorities can shift between sprints. We stay agile without being chaotic.

⬡
Sprint Planning
2-week cycles with clear goals, committed deliverables
⬟
Daily Standups
Async by default, live when needed. Full transparency
◈
Weekly Demo
Live demo of working software every Friday
◎
Code Reviews
All PRs reviewed for quality, security, and maintainability
Post-launch
04
Scale
We stay until you're ready to fly solo

Shipping is not the end. We monitor production, fix issues fast, and iterate based on real user behavior. We also run knowledge transfer sessions with your internal team so they can own the codebase confidently. We exit clean — no dependencies, no vendor lock-in.

◉
Launch Support
On-call during launch week. Zero stress for your team
⬠
Monitoring Setup
Datadog / PagerDuty alerts, dashboards, runbooks
⬡
Knowledge Transfer
Pairing sessions to transfer full codebase ownership
⬟
Documentation
Full technical docs, API references, deployment guides
What drives us

Engineering principles

01
No surprises

Bad news travels fast at Nexus. We surface problems early, communicate constantly, and never hide behind optimistic estimates. Clients deserve the truth, even when it's hard.

02
Boring technology

We reach for proven tools, not bleeding-edge hype. Postgres over the latest distributed DB. React over whatever launched last week. Reliability beats novelty.

03
Own the outcome

We're not contractors billing hours. We care about your business metrics. If something isn't working, we say so — and we change course before you have to ask.

04
Code you can own

Every line we write should be readable and maintainable by your team. We write for the engineers who come after us, not to show off.

05
Security is foundational

We don't bolt on security at the end. Threat modeling, least-privilege access, and encryption happen on day one — not the week before launch.

06
Ship, then iterate

Perfect is the enemy of shipped. We bias toward working software in production, then improve based on real usage data. Assumptions don't survive contact with users.
